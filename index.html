The short version

I grew up in Manchester. Started my first online business at 17 because I didn't want to follow the traditional path — university, corporate job, wait until 65 to enjoy life. I wanted to build something on my own terms.

It took a while to figure out. The first few months were rough — no clients, no money, no idea what I was doing. But I kept going. By 18 I was making a full-time income. By 20, I was earning over $20K a month. Now I run The Kaizen, where I help other people do the same thing.

I'm not special. I just started early and didn't stop.

Beyond the business

I'm not one of those people who only talks about business. I care about a lot of things outside of work — fitness, travel, spending time with people I care about, reading, learning new skills for the sake of it.

Manchester is home. It's where I grew up, where the business started, and where I still live. There's something about this city — the grit, the energy, the people — that keeps me grounded.

I believe in living well while you're young. Not just grinding until you burn out, but actually enjoying the process. That's what The Kaizen philosophy is about — getting better every day, in business and in life.

What matters to me

Honesty over hype

I'd rather tell someone the truth about what it takes than sell them a dream. Building a business is hard. Rewarding, but hard.

Action over theory

I've never been academic. I learn by doing. Everything I teach comes from real experience, not textbooks.

People over profit

The students who go through Skill to Profit aren't numbers. They're real people trying to change their lives. That responsibility matters to me.

Long-term thinking

I'm not building a quick cash grab. The Kaizen is meant to last. Every decision I make is about where we'll be in 5 years, not 5 months.
